Diccionario de términos

 
Air quality

The cleanliness of the air around us. Good air quality means fewer pollutants, which helps us stay healthy and protect the environment.

Biodiversity

The variety of life on Earth, including animals, plants, and microorganisms. A healthy environment has many different species living together.

Conservation

The protection and careful use of natural resources, like water, forests, and animals, to keep them from being damaged or disappearing.

Durability

How long something lasts without breaking or wearing out. Durable products are built to last longer, which helps reduce waste

Eco-friendly

Things that are good for the environment and do not harm the Earth. For example, using reusable bags instead of plastic.

Fuel-efficient

Vehicles or machines that use less fuel to travel the same distance, helping save energy and reduce pollution.

Green energy

Energy that comes from renewable sources like the sun, wind, or water, which does not pollute the environment.

Hydropower

Electricity that is produced using the energy of moving water, such as rivers or dams. It is a type of clean energy.

Impact

The effect or change something has on the environment or the world. For example, using less plastic has a positive impact on the Earth.

Jungle preservation

Protecting and keeping jungles or rainforests safe so animals, plants, and the environment can thrive.

Keen awareness

Being very aware of and understanding the importance of taking care of the planet and using resources wisely.

Landfill reduction

The process of reducing the amount of waste sent to landfills by recycling, reusing, or composting.

Materials recycling

Taking old materials, like paper or plastic, and processing them to create new products instead of throwing them away.

Nature

The natural world, including animals, plants, and landscapes. It also refers to the ecosystems that support life.

Ozone protection

Actions to protect the ozone layer, which helps shield the Earth from harmful ultraviolet rays from the sun.

Pollution control

Efforts to reduce harmful substances in the air, water, and land, such as reducing factory emissions or cleaning up oil spills.

Quality of life

How good and comfortable life is, including having clean air, water, and healthy surroundings, which are all part of sustainability.

Recycling

Turning waste materials like paper, plastic, and glass into new products instead of throwing them away.

Sustainability

Using resources in a way that meets our needs without harming the planet or using up resources for future generations.

Tree planting

The act of planting trees to help clean the air, provide homes for wildlife, and improve the environment.

Urban farming

Growing food in cities or towns, often in small spaces, to reduce the need for food transportation and promote local food sources.

Vegetarian Diet

A way of eating that does not include meat or fish, which can reduce the environmental impact of food production.

Water conservation

Using water wisely to ensure there is enough for everyone, such as turning off the tap when brushing your teeth or fixing leaks.

Xeriscaping

A landscaping method that uses drought-tolerant plants to save water, especially in areas where water is scarce.

Yellowtail

Yellowtail is a type of fish, and sustainable fishing means catching it in a way that doesn't harm the ocean or reduce fish populations.

Zero waste

A lifestyle or practice where almost all waste is recycled or reused, aiming to send nothing to the landfill.
